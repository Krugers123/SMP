# SMP Boundaries

SMP exists to improve communication bandwidth between humans and AI systems.

It must remain clearly bounded.

## Boundary Statements

- SMP is a protocol, not a model.
- SMP is an interface layer, not a weight modification method.
- SMP can describe constraints, but it does not enforce them by itself.
- SMP can carry risk information, but it does not replace risk governance.
- SMP can support agents, but it is not an autonomous agent.
- SMP can improve clarity, but it does not guarantee truth, safety, or alignment.
- SMP should keep human authority explicit in consequential contexts.

## Human Authority

SMP packets may express user intent, desired direction, and boundaries. They should not be treated as automatic permission to act in high-impact contexts.

When a decision affects safety, rights, finances, medical care, legal status, employment, security, or other consequential domains, human review and applicable governance remain required.

## Implementation Boundary

A consumer of SMP may translate packet fields into prompts, tool parameters, UI state, agent plans, or validation checks. Those translations are implementation-specific and must be documented separately.

## Public Boundary

Public SMP materials should avoid exposing private evaluation methods, sensitive policies, hidden prompts, or operational controls from any implementation.
